## 0. Why this framework, in one paragraph

Clifford (geometric) algebra is the nearest-term, highest-confidence framework
addition because it **strictly generalizes the quaternion mechanism that is
already in production**: the even subalgebra of the recommended algebra
Cl(3,0) is exactly ℍ, so the existing `quaternion_attention_torch.py` is the
restriction of the new mechanism to even-grade features — an *exact* reduction
that becomes the acceptance test, the same pattern the hyperbolic design uses
for its c → 0 Euclidean anchor (mnn.4). On top of what quaternions give
(norm-preserving rotor gating), Cl(3,0) adds graded structure (scalars,
vectors, bivectors, pseudoscalar as distinct feature *types*), the geometric
product's decomposition into inner + wedge (similarity and *oriented span* in
one associative product), and full 3D rotor semantics — while staying
associative, which the octonion mechanism deliberately gives up.

## 1. The algebra, from first principles

Cl(p,q) is the associative algebra over ℝ generated by e₁..e_{p+q} with
eᵢ² = +1 (i ≤ p), eᵢ² = −1 (i > p), and eᵢeⱼ = −eⱼeᵢ for i ≠ j. A basis is
the 2^{p+q} **blades** e_I = e_{i₁}⋯e_{i_k} (I strictly increasing); the
product of blades is a signed blade, the sign counting transpositions plus
signature contractions (Appendix A builds the table from exactly this rule).

Grading: scalar (grade 0), vectors (1), bivectors (2), …; ⟨x⟩_g projects onto
grade g. The geometric product of vectors decomposes as

    u v = u·v + u∧v        (inner: grade 0; wedge: grade 2, oriented area)

— similarity and oriented span in one product. **Reversion** x̃ reverses
factor order in each blade: grade g picks up (−1)^{g(g−1)/2} (grades 2,3 flip
sign in Cl(3,0); grades 0,1 do not). Two identities carry the whole mechanism
(both verified, A.5):

    ⟨x̃ y⟩₀ = Σ_I x_I y_I            (the ℝ^{2ⁿ} dot product IS the scalar
                                      grade of the reversed product)
    R R̃   = (Σ_I R_I²)·1            for every EVEN element R of Cl(3,0)
                                      (exactly scalar — no grade-4 part exists)

The second identity means every nonzero even element normalizes to a **rotor**
(unit versor) by dividing by √(R R̃): rotor normalization is closed-form, with
no constraint manifold to maintain. Unit rotors preserve the ℝ⁸ norm of *any*
multivector under one-sided multiplication, |R x| = |x| (A.6) — the
certificate identity.

## 2. Algebra choice: Cl(3,0), not Cl(2,0)

| criterion | Cl(2,0) | **Cl(3,0)** (recommended) |
|---|---|---|
| dimension / head_dim fit | 4 (`%4`, same as quaternion) | 8 (`%8`, proven by octonion) |
| even subalgebra | ℂ (plane rotations only) | **ℍ — the existing mechanism, exactly** |
| rotor group action | SO(2) | full SO(3) on grade-1 |
| what C1 task 5 (rotation composition) can detect | 2D phases — mostly invisible | 3D rotation composition — the task's design target |
| structure beyond quaternion | almost none (it ≅ M₂(ℝ)) | vectors + pseudoscalar as new feature types; uv = u·v + u∧v |
| multiplication cost per block | 16 mults | 64 mults (≡ dense 8×8 bilinear; same arithmetic class as octonion) |

Cl(2,0) is cheaper but adds nothing the quaternion mechanism doesn't already
do better (its even part is only ℂ, and the rotation-composition diagnostic
cannot separate it from a phase-gated baseline). **Decision: Cl(3,0)** —
8-dimensional (the Pauli algebra, ≅ M₂(ℂ)), even part exactly ℍ, pseudoscalar
e₁₂₃ central with e₁₂₃² = −1 (A.7), which gives Hodge duality
(vectors ↔ bivectors via x ↦ x·e₁₂₃) for free as an interpretive tool.

## 3. Mechanism design (the quaternion template, generalized)

The production quaternion mechanism (`quaternion_attention_torch.py`) is:
scores = plain dot product (which *is* the scalar part of q·conj(k), summed
over 4-blocks); aggregation `y_i = Q̂_i · (Σ_j w_ij · conj(K̂_j) · V_j)`,
factored by associativity into three cheap steps, with Q̂, K̂ normalized
per-block so the gating products are norm-preserving. The Clifford mechanism
is the same design with 8-blocks and the two identities of §1 doing the work:

- **Features.** head_dim is partitioned into D/8 multivector blocks of
  Cl(3,0) (`head_dim % 8 == 0`, same guard pattern as octonion). q, k, v are
  the standard linear projections, reinterpreted blockwise.
- **Scores.** Plain dot product with 1/√head_dim — by ⟨q̃ k⟩₀ = Σ q_I k_I
  this *is* the scalar grade of the reversed geometric product summed over
  blocks, so the existing fused matmul path is unchanged (zero cost, exact
  semantics; the same trick the quaternion docstring records for ℝ⁴).
- **Gates.** R(q) := even-projection of each q-block, normalized by
  √(R R̃) (exact scalar, §1); likewise R(k). Even-projection keeps the gate a
  rotor — gating is *exactly* norm-preserving, by theorem rather than by
  approximate normalization of a full multivector (whose x x̃ is not scalar).
- **Aggregation** (associativity factorization, identical shape to the
  quaternion code path):

      T_j = R̃(k_j) ∘ v_j                (blockwise geometric product)
      A_i = Σ_j w_ij · T_j               (standard attention matmul)
      y_i = R(q_i) ∘ A_i                 (one-sided rotor gate)

  ∘ is the Cl(3,0) product via a precomputed sign tensor (§6). v stays a full
  multivector: the gate rotates its grade-1 part, phase-mixes its
  {grade-0, grade-3} part, and acts on bivectors by conjugation-adjacent
  mixing — all norm-preserving.

**Reduction anchor (the F2/F3 acceptance test).** Restrict all q, k, v blocks
to the even subalgebra (odd coordinates zero). Even·even = even (A.2), the
gate's even-projection is the identity, and under the verified isomorphism
**i = e₂₃, j = −e₁₃, k = −e₁₂** (A.3: matches the implemented `qmul` table
exactly, 200 randomized checks; quaternion conjugation = Clifford reversion,
A.3b) the three aggregation steps become exactly the quaternion mechanism's
three steps. The test plants odd-zero inputs and asserts equality against
`QuaternionCausalSelfAttention` outputs on the even coordinates, with one
bookkeeping adjustment: a D-dim Clifford head holds D/8 blocks ↔ a (D/2)-dim
quaternion head holds the same D/8 quaternion channels, so the score
temperatures differ by the explicit factor √(D)/√(D/2) = √2 — the test pins
the quaternion layer's temperature accordingly. Tolerance 1e-6 fp32 (the
products are the same arithmetic in a different basis order).

## 4. What the new grades buy (the falsifiable story)

The quaternion mechanism can only represent *rotation-like* relative
transforms between tokens (unit quaternions = SU(2)). Cl(3,0) adds, per
block: a **vector channel** (grade 1) that rotors act on as genuine 3D
rotations (SO(3) sandwich semantics live inside the one-sided gate pipeline
across two layers), and a **pseudoscalar channel** that behaves as a global
orientation/chirality bit. The C1 task-5 family (rotation composition with
its dial raised to include reflections/improper elements) is the designed
detector: composing rotations *and tracking handedness* is representable in
Cl(3,0) but must be memorized by the quaternion mechanism.

## 5. Positioning

Geometric Algebra Transformer (Brehmer et al. 2023) and Clifford-group
equivariant networks (Ruhe et al. 2023) build **equivariance-first
architectures** for geometric *data* (point clouds, physics states) in
projective/spacetime algebras, with attention scores constrained to
invariants. This project differs on all three axes: (i) the object is a
**language model's** residual stream, where the algebra is a *structural
prior*, not a data symmetry — we deliberately keep plain dot-product scores
(an invariant anyway, by ⟨q̃k⟩₀) and put the algebra in the value path, the
**rotor-gate design** inherited from the production quaternion mechanism;
(ii) **certificate-first validation** — norm preservation, even-closure, and
the quaternion reduction ship as machine-checked certify rules before any
benchmark claim; (iii) the **scorecard methodology** — preregistered,
floor-gated, budget-cohorted predictions (§7) rather than benchmark deltas.

## 6. Implementation plan (F2/F3, zero further decisions)

- `nanochat/clifford_attention_torch.py`: `CliffordCausalSelfAttention
  (AttentionCore)`. Guards: `head_dim % 8 == 0`, `n_embd % 8 == 0`.
- **Blade table generation, at import time, from first principles** (no
  hardcoded 64-entry table to typo): blades as sorted index tuples; product
  sign = bubble-transposition parity, then contract equal adjacent pairs with
  signature +1 (Appendix A's exact algorithm, ~20 lines). Materialize a
  dense sign tensor `C ∈ {−1,0,+1}^{8×8×8}` with `(x∘y)_c = Σ_{a,b} C[a,b,c]
  x_a y_b`; the blockwise product is one `einsum("...a,...b,abc->...c")`.
  Property test: regenerated table == Appendix-A scratch table, associativity
  re-asserted over all 512 basis triples at import-check time in tests.
- **Grade projection** masks as constant buffers: EVEN = blades {(), (1,2),
  (1,3), (2,3)} (indices 0,4,5,6 in the canonical blade order of A).
- **Normalization**: gates R = even(x); R ← R/√(Σ_I R_I² + ε), ε = 1e-12
  (the sum IS R R̃ by A.4; ε only guards the all-zero block).
- **Initialization**: standard projection init unchanged (scores path is
  untouched dot-product attention); no rotor-specific init is needed because
  normalization makes gates unit at every scale — matching the quaternion
  mechanism, which inherits standard init for the same reason.
- **bf16 policy**: the einsum products run in fp32 islands under autocast
  (mixed ± accumulations of up to 64 terms; same policy class as
  tropical-LSE and hyperbolic-Minkowski).
- `GPTConfig` field: `clifford_record_grade_norms: bool = False` — telemetry
  of per-grade norm fractions (how much of the stream lives in vector vs
  bivector vs pseudoscalar grades), the mechanism's interpretability
  observable. **A GPTConfig field addition trips the attention-goldens
  config guard: recapture fixtures in the same commit (capture mode, diff
  verified config-only) — the fdxb lesson.** CLI: `--attention-type
  clifford`, `--clifford-record-grade-norms`.
- **Certify checks** (mechanism `clifford`): (i) blade-table associativity
  (512 triples, exact); (ii) even-closure of gate path; (iii) unit-rotor
  norm preservation |R∘x| = |x| to 1e-6 fp32 on random blocks; (iv) **the
  quaternion reduction** (§3) against the production quaternion layer;
  (v) ⟨x̃y⟩₀ == dot(x,y) (scores identity) to fp32 eps.
- **Complexity**: per 8-block, the two gate products cost 2·64 mults per
  pair-step but factor associatively to O(T) blockwise products + one
  standard T×T matmul — the same asymptotics and memory shape as the
  quaternion path (which the e2e FLOPs estimator already prices; reuse its
  accounting with the 4→8 block-size change).

## Appendix A: verification receipts (mnn.1 scratch, 2026-06-13)

Pure-python blade algebra built from the transposition-parity rule alone
(`/data/tmp/clifford_scratch.py`; reproduced in the F2 test suite):

1. **Associativity**: all 512 basis-blade triples of Cl(3,0), exact. PASS.
2. **Even-subalgebra closure**: {1, e₁₂, e₁₃, e₂₃} closed under ∘. PASS.
3. **ℍ-isomorphism**: with **i = e₂₃, j = −e₁₃, k = −e₁₂**, the blade-table
   product reproduces the *implemented* `qmul` of
   `quaternion_attention_torch.py` on 200 random pairs to 1e-12. PASS.
   (3b) Quaternion conjugation equals Clifford reversion on the even part.
   PASS. — This pins the exact basis convention F2 must use; any other sign
   choice fails the reduction test by construction.
4. **Rotor normalizer**: R R̃ is exactly scalar and equals Σ R_I² for every
   even element (no grade-4 escape in 3D), 200 random samples, 1e-12. PASS.
5. **Scores identity**: ⟨x̃ y⟩₀ = Σ_I x_I y_I for general multivectors —
   the ℝ⁸ dot product is the scalar grade of the reversed product (so the
   fused dot-product score path is exact Clifford semantics). PASS.
6. **Certificate identity**: one-sided multiplication by a unit rotor
   preserves the ℝ⁸ norm of arbitrary multivectors, 200 samples, 1e-10. PASS.
7. **Pseudoscalar**: e₁₂₃ is central and e₁₂₃² = −1 (Hodge duality
   available). PASS.
